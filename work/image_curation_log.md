# Image Curation Log

## 1. The Portfolio Image List (NanoGPT Project)
To properly showcase the NanoGPT (Nano-Llama) custom engine, my portfolio requires exactly four types of images to match the content map:
1. **Brand Icon:** The geometric monogram used for the favicon and identity.
2. **Project Architecture (Real Visual):** The Mermaid.js structural diagram of the Transformer Block.
3. **Hero Texture (Connective Tissue):** An ambient background texture to tie the portfolio together.
4. **Headshot:** An author photo for the bio section.

## 2. Real Captures vs. AI Generation
**Real Captures:**
- **Project Architecture:** I deliberately chose to use a *real screenshot* of the Markdown preview from my IDE showing the actual Transformer Block diagram (`transformer_architecture.png`). Using AI-generated images for technical architecture always results in hallucinated gibberish. The diagram accurately maps the `[B, T, C]` tensor flow.
- **Headshot:** I will use a real photo of myself. No AI-generated avatars.

**AI Generation (Connective Tissue):**
- **Brand Icon:** The hyper-minimalist geometric 'Z' logo (`z_logo_clean.png`) generated to match the technical `#171717` / `#2563EB` aesthetic.
- **Hero Texture Generation Iterations:** I iterated prompts to hold a steady, minimalist style. 
    - **Option 1 (Accepted):** A pure `#171717` background with subtle `#2563EB` abstract topographic lines. (`hero_texture_opt1.png`)
    - **Option 2 (Rejected):** A pure `#171717` background with subtle `#2563EB` abstract structural nodes. (`hero_texture_opt2.png`)

## 3. The Rejection Note (Discernment)

**Rejected Prompt:** *"A dark minimalist background. Pure #171717 color with subtle #2563EB abstract structural nodes. Clean and corporate."*

**Why I rejected Option 2:** While the visual style and color palette matched the kit perfectly, the abstract nodes and connected lines looked too much like a generic "neural network" stock photo. It distracts from the actual technical diagrams in the portfolio. I chose Option 1 (Topographic) because it provides a quiet, ambient engineering texture without directly mimicking ML architecture, leaving the actual code and diagrams to be the loudest things on the page.
