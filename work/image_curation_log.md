# Image Curation Log

## 1. The Portfolio Image List
To build out a highly technical portfolio, the visual content map requires exactly three types of images:
1. **Brand Icon:** The geometric monogram used for the favicon and identity.
2. **Project Architecture:** Visual diagrams of the ML pipelines.
3. **Code Snippets:** Core logic and tensor shape verifications.

*(Note: I explicitly rejected using a "Hero Texture" background because they inevitably compete with the work. The background will remain pure `#FAFAFA` white.)*

## 2. Real Captures vs. AI Generation
**Real Captures:**
- **Project Architecture & Code Snippets:** I will strictly use *real screenshots* of my VS Code environment and actual Mermaid.js diagrams. AI stand-ins for code look fake and instantly destroy credibility. The code must be legible and authentic.

**AI Generation (Connective Tissue):**
- **Brand Icon:** A hyper-minimalist geometric 'Z' generated to match the technical aesthetic without hallucinated text.

## 3. The Rejection Note (Discernment)
**Rejected Prompt:** *"A dark, highly technical structural background texture. Geometric wireframes, node connections..."*
**Why I rejected it:** The resulting image was an absolute nightmare of cliché "cyberpunk" nodes and hallucinated, misspelled code blocks (`optiimzer: 'adam'`). It was visually deafening and represents everything I hate about fake tech aesthetics. I scrapped the idea of a background texture entirely. An engineer's portfolio should rely on white space, typography, and actual code, not chaotic AI wallpaper.
